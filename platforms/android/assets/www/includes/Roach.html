Check In!
 

Miss May Roach began her 42 year tenure at Central State College as a professor of rural economics and school management, and eventually retired in 1956. During that time Miss Roach was responsible for establishing a conservation major on campus which led UW-Stevens Point to have the largest natural resources curriculum in the country. The building was constructed in 1964 at 301 Reserve Street, and was originally dedicated as a women's residence hall in 1965. The building is 40,147 square feet, and originally cost $681,206 to build.

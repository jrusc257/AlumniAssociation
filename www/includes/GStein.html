George Stein Building
 

The George Stein Building was constructed at 1925 Maria Drive in 1964. It was originally 238 assignable square feet, which also contained 19,904 square feet for mechanical purposes that cost $1,200,000 and houses the heating plant. In 1970, a 6,721 square foot addition, costing $220,400 was constructed to house Protective Services. In 1973, 4,170 square feet for mechanical use was added to the building, which cost $ 528,000. George V. Stein was a chief engineer from 1920 to 1954 and was the first civil servant for the building that is now named after him.

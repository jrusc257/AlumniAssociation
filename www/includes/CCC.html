The Collins Classroom Center (CCC) was named after Joseph Victor Collins, a member of the original faculty in the mathematics department. He taught mathematics here from 1894 to 1937, during both the Normal School and State Teachers College era. Dr. Collins introduced new principles of teach algebra that are still widely used throughout the teaching world. The building of the CCC began on May 10, 1965. The facility cost $1,900,000 and is comprised 52,699 assignable square feet, and is located at 1801 Fourth Avenue.

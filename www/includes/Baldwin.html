The Baldwin Residence Hall was named after Robert D. Baldwin, president from 1926 to 1930. At the age of 35, he had to fade the transition problems of changing from a Normal School to a Teachers College. Baldwin was also responsible for calling the school “Central State” and during his first year as president the first degrees were awarded at graduation. Baldwin Hall is located at 425 Isadore Street, was constructed in 1965 at a cost of $769,150, and has 36,049 square feet.

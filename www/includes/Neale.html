Neale Hall
 

Oscar W. Neale joined the Normal School in 1925, at which time he established an art course in the school curriculum. Until his retirement in 1944, he was the director of rural education at Central State College. In 1929, President Hoover appointed him to the White House Conference on Child Health. After his retirement, Neale was a member of Congress for eight years where he served on the Education and Welfare Committee. Neale Hall is located at 433 Isadore Street and was constructed in 1965 at 36,011 square feet for $768,350.
